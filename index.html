Q & A

When is the RSVP deadline?

Please RSVP as soon as you know if you will attend or not (we ask all guests to RSVP yes or no), but latest by 1st June. If something comes up and you need to change your RSVP, please reach out directly to the bride and groom.

How about COVID-19?

We're getting married on the 2nd October, and from the bottom of our hearts, we're hoping that we can have our wedding as planned (go vaccination!). If this isn't possible, we will let you know how this affects the setup of our wedding.

Can I bring a date?

Guests are indicated on the received invitation.

Are kids welcome?

Our wish is to celebrate this day with adults only. If you have infants that can't spend time away from their parents, they are of course welcome.

Is the wedding indoors or outdoors?

Both the ceremony and reception will be indoors, but please bring "kläder efter väder" (choose clothes based on the weather) since you will walk from the church to the boat.

Can I take the car to the church?

Since we're going from the church to the reception/party by boat, we strongly recommend not taking the car this day. There is however parking available both at the church and at Ekensdal.

What time should I arrive?

The ceremony will start at 14.30 sharp, please be well on time.

Can I take photos during the wedding ceremony?

We love photos! That is why we have hired a really good wedding photographer to take pictures throughout the day, and to not disturb her in her work we would like to ask you bring out your phone during the wedding ceremony. During the party however, go wild! :)

Are there any Swedish wedding traditions I should be aware of?

As a Swede, it's difficult to point out which traditions that are specifically Swedish and which are international. However, here are a few key elements:

Brudskål. The first toast of the day/evening is called the Brudskål (bride toast). This will for this specific wedding happen on the boat from Nybrokajen to Ekensdal. Don’t taste the champagne before this first toast.

Sit-down dinner. The sit-down dinner is long (but has breaks). Be prepared to sit for a good 3-4 hours, hopefully entertained by toastmasters and speakers! If you want to say a few words, sing something, or maybe do a dance performance – you need to let the toastmasters know well in advance before the wedding. No spontanous “spontantal” or “spontansång” at weddings, those are reserved for other events.

You may now (all) kiss the bride. When the bride leaves the room it’s traditional for all the women to line up to kiss the groom (on the cheek). And when the groom leaves the room to go to the bathroom then all the men get up and kiss the bride. So, don’t be alarmed if this happens!

A wedding is a party. This means that we will party once the dinner is finished, and we have reserved a bus to take you all back to the city when we close at Ekensdal at 02.00. We're hoping that you're all still there to sing along to the last song (Stad i Ljus by Tommy Körberg).

Swedish Music Do you just hate the feeling when everyone around you seems to LOVE a song that you don’t even know? Well, we do have a lot of Swedish songs that might create this feeling for you if you're not Swedish. If you want to avoid this (to some extent) by listening in beforehand, so that you at least have heard them once, we recommend to add the following songs to your Spotify playlist: “Stad I ljus – Tommy Körberg”, “Vart jag mig I världen vänder – Den Svenska Björnstammen”, “Kung för en dag – Magnus Uggla” etc.
